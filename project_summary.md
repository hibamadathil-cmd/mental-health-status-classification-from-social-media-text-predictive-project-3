\# Project Summary



\## Project: Mental Health Status Classification from Social Media Text

\## Course: Predictive Analytics 

\## Problem Statement

Mental health conditions affect millions worldwide. Early detection

through social media text analysis can help identify at-risk individuals

and provide timely intervention.



\## Objective

Classify social media posts into 5 mental health categories:

\- Stress

\- Depression

\- Bipolar Disorder

\- Personality Disorder

\- Anxiety Disorder



\## Methodology

1\. Data Collection — Reddit mental health posts

2\. Preprocessing — cleaning, tokenization, stop word removal

3\. EDA — class distribution, word clouds, text length analysis

4\. Feature Engineering — TF-IDF, BERT embeddings

5\. Model Training — TF-IDF+SVM, BERT fine-tuning

6\. Evaluation — accuracy, F1, sensitivity, specificity

7\. Explainability — LIME, TF-IDF weights, word clouds

8\. Deployment — Streamlit web application



\## Key Results

\- TF-IDF + SVM: 81.3% accuracy, 81.4% F1 score

\- LIME explanations provide per-prediction transparency



\## Limitations

\- Dataset may not represent all demographics

\- Social media text is noisy and informal

\- Model not intended for clinical diagnosis



\## Future Work

\- Include more mental health categories

\- Use larger BERT models

\- Add multilingual support
