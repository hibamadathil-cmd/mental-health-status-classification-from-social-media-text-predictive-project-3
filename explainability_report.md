\# Explainability Report



\## Project: Mental Health Status Classification from Social Media Text



\## Methods Used



\### 1. LIME (Local Interpretable Model-Agnostic Explanations)

\- Explains individual predictions

\- Shows which words pushed prediction toward each class

\- Green words = positive contribution

\- Red words = negative contribution



\### 2. TF-IDF Feature Weights

\- Global feature importance

\- Shows most discriminative words per class

\- Helps understand model decision boundaries



\### 3. Word Clouds

\- Visual representation of class-specific vocabulary

\- Generated per mental health category

\- Larger words = higher frequency/importance



\## Key Findings



\### Stress

\- Key words: work, deadline, pressure, tired, overwhelmed



\### Depression

\- Key words: hopeless, empty, sad, lonely, worthless



\### Bipolar Disorder

\- Key words: energy, mood, episode, manic, cycle



\### Personality Disorder

\- Key words: identity, relationship, unstable, impulsive



\### Anxiety Disorder

\- Key words: worry, panic, fear, nervous, anxious



\## Ethical Considerations

\- Model not intended for clinical diagnosis

\- Predictions should be verified by professionals

\- Privacy of users must be protected

\- Bias in training data may affect predictions
